Color Foundation

The complete color language of the system — a calm, warm, paper-first palette anchored by a muted blue-grey brand and a single confident gold accent. Built for clarity, restraint and longevity.

01 — Philosophy

How the color groups work together

Four groups carry distinct responsibilities. Kept in their lanes, they produce an interface that feels editorial and quiet — never loud — while still giving the eye a clear path through actions and meaning.

Brand · muted blue-grey

The signature. Used sparingly for primary actions, focus, links and identity. Its low saturation keeps the product calm — it guides rather than shouts.

Neutrals · warm paper

The stage. Warm off-whites and beige form ~84% of every screen, giving the brand and accent room to breathe and making long reading comfortable.

Accent · gold

The spark. A single warm gold for highlights, marks and moments of emphasis. Capped at ~2% of the canvas so it always reads as intentional.

Functional · status

The signals. Success, warning, error and info are desaturated to live inside the warm world, communicating state without breaking the calm.

07 — Accessibility

Contrast & safe pairings

The palette is light and warm, so contrast must be checked deliberately. Ratios below are approximate WCAG 2.1 values.

Text Primary on Paper
Body & headings — the default pairing.
#403636 on #FBFAF6≈ 9.6:1 · AAA
Secondary on Paper
Supporting copy & captions.
#6F6966 on #FBFAF6≈ 4.9:1 · AA
Inverse on Brand-900
Footers, dark panels, tooltips.
#FFFFFF on #2F383C≈ 10.4:1 · AAA
Ink on Gold
The only correct text color on accent.
#403636 on #F2CB57≈ 7.0:1 · AAA
⚠ Avoid: white on Primary
#FFF on #8D9FA6 ≈ 2.1:1 — fails. For white text use Brand-700 #55666C (≈ 5.6:1) or put text.primary on the brand instead.
⚠ Avoid: Muted as body text
#A6A19D on paper ≈ 2.3:1 — decorative & large-only. Never use Muted for paragraphs or small labels that must be read.
